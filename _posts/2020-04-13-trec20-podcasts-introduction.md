# 前言

> TREC 2020出现了一个新的track，就是Podcasts Track。虽然网页上还没有太多的信息，但是根据之前转发给所有参与者的一封邮件，这篇文章总结一下目前了解到的一些（可能）信息。



# 关于Podcasts Track

今年的TREC（https://trec.nist.gov/pubs/call2020.html）也是拥有多个track，每一个track对应着不同的具体任务。其中TREC 2020加入了最新的Podcasts Track，其宗旨是为了更好地从open-domain podcast中来执行信息检索功能。

在4月10日Podcasts Track的组织者给所有参赛者发送了一封邮件，在邮件中给出了关于这个track的更多信息。同时根据邮件，本周会release需要的数据。（https://sites.google.com/view/podcastsdataset/home?authuser=0）



## 可能的query？

根据邮件，对于podcast tracks的query可能和news track或其他过往TREC中使用的query相似。但是为了突出podcast自身的特点，可能的query会充分考虑podcast听众的需求。具体来说，会期待是一个**2-3个词的query但是包含更多的背景信息**。

> We don't expect that typically to be "tell me everything about my_favorite_topic​" but more like "give me a single podcast to listen to on my_favorite_topic".



## 任务

### 任务1: 排序

根据给出的query，返回一段片段，能最好的对应query的信息。

> given a query return fixed-length segments relevant to the query



### 任务2: 概括podcast

根据给出的podcast，概括出podcast中出现的信息。

> given a podcast episode return a short text summary



## 返回的结果

针对每个query，期待返回的结果是一个对于片段的排序列表。这里的片段可能是来自不同的podcast文件。具体对于这个**片段**， 更多可以理解为是一个听众开始收听的**起始点**。所以这个返回的结果列表其实是一个对于带有不同**收听起始点**的podcast的排序。

> We want participants to return a ranking of segments, which can be a potpourri across different podcasts. Each item in the list would be interpreted as the jump-in point where the listener would start listening to that podcast episode. The list represents a ranking of possible podcast episodes with jump-in points, but we would expect a common use-case would be for the listener to browse the descriptions and pick just one to listen to. Our hypothetical listener might then listen more than 2 minutes, but that's the granularity we'll use for judging this year.



## 怎样衡量

### 对排序的衡量

类似于使用**nDCG** (Discounted cumulative gain)来评价排序的质量。



### 对于summarization的衡量

1. 和ROUGE想法类似的，让podcast制作方和TREC参与者来评判summarization的质量。
2. embedding-based的对summarization进行衡量。

> as discussed in the call, something nDCGish on the ranked list of segments for ad-hoc retrieval, and for summarization we are getting more expert advice but are thinking of happy a couple of metrics: (1) something like ROUGE on summaries judged as high-quality, with a mix of those provided by podcast creators, and those generated by TREC participants, as well as (2) an embedding-based approach to evaluation summarization.
